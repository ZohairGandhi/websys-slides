Boolean Algebra

  • In Boolean algebra, variables can have one of two values: true (1) or false (0)
  • It uses logical operators such as:
    • conjunction (AND), denoted as $\wedge$
    • disjunction (OR), denoted as $\vee$
    • negation (NOT), denoted as $\neg$

Basic Operations

Operator Notation Alternative Notation Definition
AND $x \wedge y$ $x \cdot y, xy$ Only true when both x and y are true
OR $x \vee y$ $x + y$ Only false when both x and y are false
NOT $\neg x$ $X$ Inverts the value of x

Boolean Algebra Laws

  • Boolean algebra satisfies many ordinary algebra laws when considering $\vee$ as addition and $\wedge$ as multiplication
Associativity of $\vee$: $x \vee (y \vee z) = (x \vee y) \vee z$
Associativity of $\wedge$: $x \wedge (y \wedge z) = (x \wedge y) \wedge z$
Commutativity of $\vee$: $x \vee y = y \vee x$
Commutativity of $\wedge$: $x \wedge y = y \wedge x$
Distributivity of $\wedge$ over $\vee$: $x \wedge (y \vee z) = (x \wedge y) \vee (x \wedge z)$
Identity for $\vee$: $x \vee 0 = x$
Identity for $\wedge$: $x \wedge 1 = x$
Annihilator for $\wedge$: $x \wedge 0 = 0$

Boolean Algebra Laws

  • Some laws only hold in Boolean algebra, not in ordinary algebra
Annihilator for $\vee$: $x \vee 1 = 1$
Distributivity of $\vee$ over $\wedge$: $x \vee (y \wedge z) = (x \vee y) \wedge (x \vee z)$

De Morgan's Laws

De Morgan 1 $\neg x \wedge \neg y = \neg (x \vee y)$
De Morgan 2 $\neg x \vee \neg y = \neg (x \wedge y)$
